The Band

For the release of the first album Grohl brought in bass player Nate Mendel, drummer William Goldsmith and second guitarist Pat Smear. The band's first single "This Is A Call" was released in June 1995. Taylor Hawkins replaced Goldsmith as drummer in 1997. The band now includes Chris Shiflett on guitar and Rami Jafree on keyboard.

Dave Grohl - Lead Vocals (1994-present)

(born January 14, 1969)

An American singer, songwriter, musician and director. He has been the founder, frontman, lead vocalist, rhythm guitarist and primary songwriter of the Foo Fighters since 1994, and was the best-known and longest-serving drummer for Nirvana from 1990 to 1994.

Taylor Hawkins - Drummer (1997-present)

(born February 17, 1972)

An American musician, best known as the drummer of the rock band Foo Fighters. Prior to joining the band in 1997, he was the touring drummer for Alanis Morissette as well as the drummer in a progressive experimental band called Sylvia. He was voted "Best Rock Drummer" in 2005 by the UK drumming magazine Rhythm. Hawkins is also an accomplished rock singer, guitarist and pianist. Many Foo Fighters live shows feature Hawkins singing lead vocals on at least one cover song.

Nate Mendel - Bass Guitarist (1995-present)

(born December 2, 1968)

An American musician, singer and songwriter. He has been a member of Foo Fighters, The Jealous Sound, Sunny Day Real Estate, and The Fire Theft during his career. He plays bass guitar, double bass, guitar, violin, keyboards, drums, and vocals.

Pat Smear - Guitarist (1995-1997, 2005-present)

(born August 5, 1959)

Georg Albert Ruthenberg is better known by the stage name Pat Smear. An American musician and actor. He was a founding member of punk band the Germs, and was the touring guitarist for grunge band Nirvana from 1993 to 1994. When Grohl set up the Foo Fighters, Smear joined briefly on guitar, and after taking time on solo projects re-joined in 2005. He has recorded four studio albums and a live album with the band.

Chris Shiflett - Guitarist (1999 - present)

(born May 6, 1971)

Best known as the lead guitarist for the Foo Fighters since June 1999, and the punk rock band No Use for a Name, as well as the punk rock cover band Me First and the Gimme Gimmes. He is the younger brother of musician Scott Shiflett.

Rami Jafree - Keyboard (2017 - present)

(born March 11, 1969)

An American musician who is the keyboardist for Foo Fighters and the Wallflowers. He has worked for many artists including Pete Yorn, Soul Asylum, Stone Sour, Joseph Arthur and Coheed and Cambria. He plays Keyboards, accordion, and harmonica.
