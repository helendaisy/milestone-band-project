If you have somehow avoided the global phenomenon that is the Foo Fighters, they are an American rock band, formed in Seattle, Washington, in 1994. It was founded by Nirvana drummer Dave Grohl as a one-man project following the dissolution of Nirvana.

(Fun Fact) The group got its name from the UFOs and various aerial phenomena that were reported by Allied Aircraft Pilots in World War II, which were known collectively as "foo fighters".

For the release of the first album Grohl brought in bass player Nate Mendel, drummer William Goldsmith and second guitarist Pat Smear. The band's first single "This Is A Call" was released in June 1995. Taylor Hawkins replaced Goldsmith as drummer in 1997.

The band have released 9 studio albums, the most recent Concrete and Gold in September 2017.

Foo Fighters have sold out numerous arena and stadium tours across the world and headlined all the great festivals including Glastonbury and Coachella.

The band now includes Chris Shiflett on guitar and Rami Jafree on keyboard. Head to the bio page to find out more about each member.
